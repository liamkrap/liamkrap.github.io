ABOUT ME

Hi, my name is Liam, I'm a Uni student and the Australian Catholic University in Strathfield. I'm currently studying a Bachlor of Education Secondary and Bachelor of Arts (Design Innovation and Technologies), which will allow me to be a high school TAS teacher. I'm majoring in Industrial Technology (Timber and Metal) and minoring in Textiles and Digital Technologies. I've worked with Primary School aged students for 8 years, so I'm looking forward to working with High School aged students and the different challenges and rewards that come with that age group.

I'm passionate about all things relating to design, and particularly enjoy the design process; identifying a need, proposing a solution, endless ideation, reflection, evaluation, and finally the physical realisation of a product. Within this, I find the ideation process to be the most enjoyable, I have always been fond of sketching, and I think it's amazing to look back at how an idea came to be through countless versions and adjustments.

Throughout my studies I have developed my knowledge and skills within the workshop, building proficiencies with many tools and practices. On the right you can see a list of tools that I am familiar with at varying levels. Not listed is CAD, my greatest achievement is my knowledge and skill within Fusion360. Over the past three years I have spent a lot of time self-directing my own learning within the program, so that I can use it to its full potential.

So far, I have completed three 3-week placements, with one remaining in 2026 which will be 7 weeks. Below it is a list of the schools I was able to learn from and the disciplines which I taught in. Interestingly, my experience has only been with single-gender schools - all girls at Roseville and Mercy, and all boys at Holy Cross. I'm looking forward to the day that I get to experience teaching at a co-ed school as it will again be a different dynamic.
